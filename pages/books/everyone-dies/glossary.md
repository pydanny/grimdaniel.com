## Alinam, City

A large city on the Great River delta leading into Alimam bay.

## Arming Sword

A straight, two-edged sword good for cuts and thrusts. The predecessor of the longsword, and the traditional sword of the Melikki people. Forbidden for commoners to use and is part of the heraldry of the Kingdom of Namayan. Still used by traditionalists, but being supplanted by the longsword.

## Creator

The great spirit or diety who created the world. Since this momentous event, they sleep in the Quiet Lands, where the spirits of the dead are supposed to rest until the Creator awakens at the end of time.

## Falchion

A usually single-edged one-handed sword with one straight side and the other being curved out. Usually made with a proper hilt. Great for cutting. A step up from a knife-hilted sword, but not the same as an arming sword.

## Longsword

Directly analagous to the longsword of 13th-15th century Europe, this is an approximately four foot long weapon with a straight, pointed blade and two handed hilt. Best used with both hands. Sometimes called a "bastard sword" or "hand-and-a-half sword".

## Knife-hilted swords

Swords with knife-style hilts, cheaper than normal hilts and often used by commoners. Also called 'messer' swords

## Kingdom of Namayan

The land in which the "Everyone Dies" series takes place. The nation of Namayan sits in a large, flat plain, bordered to the North by the White Mountains, to the west by a saltwater Alinam bay on which the city of Alinam sits, to the East by the freshwater Great Lake, and by the South the Great River.

Royalty and nobles of the kingdom claim to be descendants of the Melikki sea nomads who conquered the Old Empire. The heraldic symbol of the kingdom is an arming sword pointing up through a crown.

Recently the Kingdom of Namayan fought a long war with the Kingdom of the Lake, the nation on the other ride of the Great River. 


## Kingdom of the Lake

The southern neighbor of the Kingdom of Namayan, the border being the Great River. Like Namayan, the upper classes are of Melikki heritage.

People of Namayan often refer to people in this kingdom as 'Southrons'.

## Melikki

A seafaring race of warlike nomads. They brought down the weakened Old Empire and took over. The upper classes are mostly of this stock, and marriage is prohibited.

## Nataab

The mountainous land across the Alinam Bay. Sparsely populated, heavily wooded, and very rugged terrain.

## Necromancers

As Wizards focus on the learning about creation, Necromancers focus on mastery over spirits, death, shadows, and endings. A common goal of this knowledge is to become immortal.

Necromancers can command spirits to reanimate bodies, creating undead slaves. They can focus the various powers of death and endings to encourage rot and decay. They often lean into wizardry to change their bodies and those of their minions into weapons. Shadow magic is a subset of necromancy with its own strange set of powers trying to the Realm of Darkness that existed before the Creator made the world.

Rarely, necromancers have learned to transition their spirits to new bodies as old ones age or become injured. This is considered a vile act, as it involves stealing a person's body, and doing so means the person is now a "lich". 

Necromancers learn from each other, from angry spirits who want to hurt the world, and books.

## Old Empire

An empire that came from across the sea in great ships powered by powerful magics. A relic of their power is the School of Wizardry in the Great Lake. During a brutal war with another power, the lands described in this series was invading by Melikki sea nomads. Fighting for survival and unable to properly support their colony, the Empire left the area.

## Poleax

A five- or six-foot long ironshod staff with a complicated head that includes an axe for chopping and hooking, a pick for punching through armor, and a spike for stabbing. Used often on the field of battle and never carried into court or polite company.


## Witches

Sometimes called 'hedge wizards,' witches learn magical lore roughly similar but do not delve into Creator lore.

Witches have similar powers to wizards but because their training is less formal tend to focus on more practical uses of magic. This means fertility and protective charms, house magic like animating things to do work, weather magic, and control of animals. 

Witches learn their craft from other witches.

## Wizards

Also called "mages", wizards touch the forces of creation, but cannot in themselves create anything that isn't ephemeral. They have deep knowledge of the Creator, and master the Old Speech that the Creator used to make the world. They spend much of their time thinking about the intentions of the Creator, as that is what is considered the font of wisdom in the world.

By mastering the true names of things Wizards can control and in some cases change those things. Typical powers include protections and wards, encouraging nature to do what it wants to do (rain, avalanche, water springs, etc), controlling of animals, control of people who have foolishly given up their true names, personal shapechanging, generation of light or illusions or wind or fire, destroying of magical deceptions, and seeing into the Quiet Lands.

Wizards learn their art on the Island of Knowledge in the Great Lake, which takes students from all nations. Students who don't earn the wizard's staff are called 'Sorcerers,' and are generally easier to find than full wizards.
