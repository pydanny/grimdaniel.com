## Brutalist Architecture in Manila

I'm a fan of [brutalist architecture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brutalist_architecture). Elements of brutalism will feature in the city-based setting of my next book, "Soldier of the Dead Gods". 

For reference, I currently live in an area of Manila, called Makati, with a good number of classic structures built in this style. Many of them are absolutely fascinating in design.

In theory, brutalism is a perfect design for this environment, as the concrete provides a good insulating medium against the heat that dominates the region. However, the humidity and smog means that concrete shows stains more quickly from water and pollution than modernist constructions of glass and steel.

Here's an example of brutalism: the Country Space 1 building on Senator Gil Puyat Avenue in Makati City. The building narrows as it goes up. A lot of effort is spent keeping it clear of water stains. It's a pleasant change from the normal blocky design of steel and glass towers that dominate the architecture landscape here.
