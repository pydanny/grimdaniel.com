<p>Hey there,</p>
<p>I'm not dead yet. Unlike the unfortunate characters in my stories, I'm alive and writing. As you may know, I write fantasy in the grimdark genre.</p>

<p>One of the things about my stories is that I try to focus on realistic yet easily read battle scenes. I've done quite a bit of weapons-based martial arts with sparring (kendo, fencing, HEMA, FMA) that I have to lean away from too much jargon and detail. On the other hand, this knowledge gives me the ability to lean into the real-world concepts of reach, fitness, and injury. I feel these three things are so important for describing battle in the dark fiction I am writing these days.</p>

<p>Specifically, in grimdark battle scenes, things tend to lean towards being realistic and gritty. In the real world, spears beat swords (reach), endurance can beat skill (fitness), and almost anything that penetrates armor ends a fight (injury).</p>

<p>In contrast, often in typical fantasy the trio of reach, fitness, and injury just aren't important. Typically swords and knives are the ultimate close range weapons, endurance is rarely an issue for trained warriors, and injuries can be shrugged off with the will to do so. This isn't to say those books are bad, but it is rare for me when reading those normal fantasy to feel as if protagonists are in any real danger. Of course, I still like those books, they just aren't "gritty".</p>

<p>For me, part of the fun of writing "Everyone Dies" was leaning into the impact of fighting in a concise, exciting way. Reach matters in that protagonists try to hit opponents before they themselves get hit. Endurance is an issue for the longer lived protagonists. And the first injury a protagonist suffers is often the end for them. The trick was covering these three topics without boring the reader with too much technical jargon. Bogging the reader down in the swamp of unnecessary detail is a sin I try to avoid. Instead I tried to focus on the dynamism and desperation of life-and-death conflict. I like to think that with "Everyone Dies" I succeeded.</p>
