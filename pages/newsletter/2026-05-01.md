<p>Hey there,</p>
<p>I'm not dead yet. Unlike the unfortunate characters in my stories, I'm alive and writing. As you may know, I write fantasy in the grimdark genre.</p>

<p>When there's fighting in my writing I focus on battle scenes that feel real but stay readable. My experience with weapons-based martial arts (kendo, fencing, HEMA, FMA) helps. However, I have to be careful to avoid jargon and instead lean on three fundamentals: reach, fitness, and injury.</p>

In the real world these things matter. Spears beat swords (reach), endurance can outweigh skill (fitness), and a single penetrating hit through armor often ends the fight (injury).

In more traditional fantasy, these factors are often minimized—knives beat swords, heroes fight tirelessly, shrug off wounds, and face little real danger. Those stories can still be great, but they aren’t gritty.

With "Everyone Dies", I aimed to show combat where these realities shape every fight: striking first matters, exhaustion is real, and one wound can be dehibilitating or fatal. The challenge was conveying this without bogging things down in technical detail. Instead, I focused instead on urgency, motion, and desperation. In other words, the "emotion" or "feeling" of the fight.</p>
