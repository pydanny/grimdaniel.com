## About Everyone Dies

Years ago, I co-wrote a book in which the protagonist died at the end. I liked the protagonist, so did my co-author, and so did the readers. So, at the beginning of the sequel, we brought the character back to life.

While it was fun to write that resurrection, it did mean that his sacrifice was lessened. He had paid the ultimate price, but then got a refund.

Since then, I kept thinking about writing a new book (or series) where protagonists die with regularity. Sacrifice would have meaning. Characters would struggle with the death of those around them. I would promise that death is permanent.

The result is this story.

The release of this book is bittersweet. I admit that I don't want to let some of the characters go. There's so much more I could write about them. I certainly have my favorites, and I've considered writing prequels. Or perhaps in the books to come, I'll have people discuss these characters the way we do in the real world, cherish the memories of friends and family who have passed away.

## Description of Everyone Dies

- Aaron, a mercenary archer with a murderous past.
- Bram, a man who lives by winning contests of skill.
- Timo, a quiet woodcutter with no taste for war.
- Melody, a young swordswoman desperate to prove she belongs.
- Trent, a weary soldier awaiting the birth of his great-grandchild.
- Konstantina, a fire mage sworn to the king's service.
- Shai, a seer cursed with the knowledge of what is to come.

They travel together, bound by circumstance, loyalty, and fragile hope. But their fates are already sealed. Because in the end...

Everyone dies.

A grimdark fantasy for adult readers containing violence, betrayal, and despair.

## Please write a review!

Yup, here's where I ask for reviews. Even the negative ones.

Positive reviews inspire me to write more. I run to the keyboard and the words and story rush out. They energize me and I love each and every one I get.

Negative reviews fuel the negative emotions that are useful for portraying the darkness in the stories I write. Also, they help me identify what I can improve. So it is better for me to have dozens of negative reviews than no reviews at all.

Even neutral reviews are good. I feel neutral about them, but having them is better than no review!

No matter how you rate "Everyone Dies", you help me by writing them.
