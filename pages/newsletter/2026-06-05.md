## Everyone Dies post-launch report

On May 20th I released my first new fiction book in over six years, "[Everyone Dies](https://storyoriginapp.com/universalbooklinks/8c487a18-2b3e-11f1-aa1d-dfa5b3422a69)", on Amazon. Here's how it has done in terms of sales:

- 11 Preorders
- 21 Paid Orders
- 128 Kindle Unlimited Pages Read (I subtracted all three times I re-read the book)
- 198 Free Orders

This is just the start in terms of getting people to read my book. Getting the book out was an invaluable learning experience. I revisited how to format books and explored the new and different world of author promotion. 

As for the reception, [Everyone Dies](https://storyoriginapp.com/universalbooklinks/8c487a18-2b3e-11f1-aa1d-dfa5b3422a69) is doing quite well in terms of score, although it could use a lot more reviews and ratings:

- <a href="https://storyoriginapp.com/universalbooklinks/8c487a18-2b3e-11f1-aa1d-dfa5b3422a69">Amazon: 4.5 stars on 8 reviews</a>
- <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/250190086-everyone-dies">Goodreads: 4.36 stars on 14 ratings</a>

## Uma finishes 2nd grade

On Wednesday our daughter Uma had her last day of school, which per the modern era is a concert/graduation ceremony. She attended a new school for the 2026-2027 academic year. Previously she had been schooled in London and last year we moved to Manila. She made a lot of new friends, learned a lot of new things, and grew quite a bit. We're very proud of her.

## [Curse of Infiniti](https://dl.bookfunnel.com/w3wkv7akr5?tid=3ie8f6lbal)

Waking up without memory in fantasy and science fiction is trope I find fascinating. Years ago I wrote a grimdark science fiction story which had a similar beginning, and Michael Fletcher's Obsidian Path ([reviewed here](https://grimdaniel.com/reviews/obsidian-path)) starts in a similar way. It is interesting reading how Rachel Hetrick handles it in her story, [Curse of Infiniti](https://dl.bookfunnel.com/w3wkv7akr5?tid=3ie8f6lbal), listed below. While the author doesn't list her story as dark fantasy, I think aspects of it definately lean into the genre.
