## Everyone Dies post-launch report

On May 20th I released my first new fiction book in over six years, "[Everyone Dies](https://storyoriginapp.com/universalbooklinks/8c487a18-2b3e-11f1-aa1d-dfa5b3422a69)", on Amazon. Here's how it has done in terms of sales:

- 11 Preorders
- 21 Paid Orders
- 128 Kindle Unlimited Pages Read (I subtracted all three times I re-read the book)
- 198 Free Orders

In terms of reception, [Everyone Dies](https://storyoriginapp.com/universalbooklinks/8c487a18-2b3e-11f1-aa1d-dfa5b3422a69) is doing quite well (although it could use a lot more reviews and ratings):

- <a href="https://storyoriginapp.com/universalbooklinks/8c487a18-2b3e-11f1-aa1d-dfa5b3422a69">Amazon: Review average of 4.5 stars</a>
- <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/250190086-everyone-dies">Goodreads: Rating average of 4.36 stars</a>

## The darkness and parenting

Our daughter Uma has gotten deep into reading chapter books this year. She is very curious about Everyone Dies, but the book isn't appropriate for a seven-year-old.

If you've read a bit of my fiction writing, you know that it can be quite dark and heavy in places. You get to know the people in my head, and sometimes brutal battles with fantastical creatures kill characters right after you've developed a deep attachment to them.

So Uma has been asking for a kid's version of the story, with suggested renames like "Everyone Cries" and "Everyone Fries." Parents out there, have you ever rewritten a book or created an alternative version for your children?

## Starting without memory

Waking up without memory in fantasy and science fiction is a trope I find fascinating. I'm in the midst of rewriting a grimdark science fiction story which had a similar beginning, and Michael Fletcher's Obsidian Path ([reviewed here](https://grimdaniel.com/reviews/obsidian-path)) also has this start. It is interesting reading how Rachel Hetrick handles it in her story, [Curse of Infiniti](https://dl.bookfunnel.com/w3wkv7akr5?tid=3ie8f6lbal), listed below. This is a very interesting dark fantasy story, with lurid descriptions of the physical world and the mind of the protagonist.
